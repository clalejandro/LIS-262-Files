PHNOG 2018 Conference Experience

By: Christine Alejandro, March 12, 2018

Philippines Network Operators Group (PhNOG) held its PHNOG 2018 conference last March 5 at the I'M Hotel in Makati. It was attended by various network operators around the country. There are also international delegates who attended the conference.

Philippines Network Operators Group (PhNOG) is a non-profit organization established to promote coordination among Network Operators in Philippines. Focus is given to knowledge development of all members as well as the Philippines IT community as a whole through discussions on technical issues/concerns regarding the Internet and network management. [Source: BNS hosting]

The conference program started with the singing of the Philippine National Anthem. It was followed by the introduction and opening remarks from the organizers represented by Mr. Achie Atienza and Mr. Benjie Sevilla respectively.

There were recorded video messages from leaders of different ICT related institutions here and abroad. These were from Department of Information and Communications Technology (DICT), Internet Society (ISOC), Asia-Pacific Network Information Centre (APNIC) and Bandwidth and Signal Statistics (BASS) Project.

Updates from PhNOG, APNIC and Philippine Open Interchange (PhOpenIX) were delivered by their respective representatives.

After lunch, there were other talks about different topics like China Carrier update, update on National broadband project in the Philippines and Pinoy basketball website.

All throughout the conference there were raffle of prizes made possible by the generous sponsors.
